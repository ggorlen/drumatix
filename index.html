My 606 story

I first heard the 606 when my best friend in high school gave me recordings by his brother, Lou Gigger. The snappy, aggressive sound of the TR-606 and Lou's busy, skittering programming had me hooked. It's been my favorite drum machine since.

Compared to other machines like the 808 and 909, the 606 has a tinnier, crisper, less thumpy sound. I tend to associate it more with underground music than the A-level productions the 808 and 909 are known for. A 606 sounds great with distortion and on tape.

The 80s and 90s are probably the prime periods for 606 recordings. During the 80s, most of the 606 music I know of that use the 606 are industrial, minimal synth and cassette culture recordings. During the 90s, IDM seems to be the genre that most featured the 606.

Other drum machines I associate with underground 80s music are the Korg KR-55, Boss DR-55, Roland TR-707 and Roland CR-8000. There are many brilliant recordings featuring these machines and I'd like to list them here eventually.

I used to own a 606 and made a couple tapes with it but eventually sold it along with most of my music gear.

My favorite 606 recordings

  • Lego Feet - S/T

    This Autechre alias is the holy grail of IDM TR-606 for me. A few tracks don't have the 606, but 80% of the album is pure Roland MC-202 and 606. The programming pushes the 606 to the limits.

  • Autechre - Bike

    Fantastic, driving 606 IDM classic that kicks off Incunabula.

  • Autechre - Egg

    Basically a demo version of the beautiful "Eggshell" from Incunabula with a 606 and Gescom-style cut-up vocals. This track nicely illustrates how cool the 606 sounds with rolling accented snares. The 606 is augmented with percussion samples, so it's not the most distilled example of the machine.

  • Portion Control - With Mixed Emotion

    One of my favorite 80s minimal synth tapes. A great example of saturated and disorted 606.
